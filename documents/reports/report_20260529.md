## Key Findings

### 1. Multi-temporal vs Single-date

Multi-temporal experiments consistently outperform single-date by a large margin:

| Group | avg test mIoU | ΔmIoU vs single-date |
|---|---|---|
| Single-date (GSI+RF, both archs) | 0.570 | — |
| Naive MT (GSI+RF k3, both archs) | 0.660 | +0.090 |
| GSI Direct k5–k10 (both archs) | 0.676 | +0.106 |
| RF Direct k5–k10 (both archs) | 0.687 | +0.117 |

**+9–12 pp mIoU** from adding temporal context. Alfalfa and Winter Wheat benefit most (+18–25 pp IoU) — both have distinct phenological windows not visible in a single date.

### 2. Band Selection Method: GSI vs RF

At equivalent k:

| k | GSI DeepLab | RF DeepLab | GSI SegFormer | RF SegFormer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5 | 0.6783 | 0.6782 | 0.6732 | 0.6770 |
| 10 | 0.6769 | **0.6959** | 0.6740 | 0.6955 |

- At k=5: GSI ≈ RF (difference < 0.001)
- At k=10: RF outperforms GSI by +0.019 pp (DeepLab) and +0.022 pp (SegFormer)
- RF captures non-linear importance; GSI is a simpler separability score — difference widens at higher k

### 3. Effect of K (channels per crop)

| Selector | k | DeepLab mIoU | SegFormer mIoU |
|---|---|---|---|
| GSI | 5 | 0.6783 | 0.6732 |
| GSI | 10 | 0.6769 | 0.6740 |
| RF | 5 | 0.6782 | 0.6770 |
| RF | 10 | 0.6959 | 0.6955 |

- For GSI: k=5 and k=10 near identical (~0.001 difference) — GSI saturates early
- For RF: k=10 meaningfully better than k=5 (+0.018 pp) — RF benefits from wider channel pool

### 4. Architecture Comparison

SegFormer slightly faster to converge (lower epochs); DeepLabV3+ slightly higher peak mIoU on multi-temporal configs. Difference is small (<0.002 pp) across most experiments — both architectures respond similarly to band selection quality.

Exception: RF k10 nearly identical (DeepLab 0.6959, SegFormer 0.6955).

### 5. Per-Class Analysis

| Class | Single-date avg IoU | Best MT IoU | Main challenge |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rice | 0.902 | 0.933 | Flooded fields distinctive in NIR; already high single-date |
| Tomatoes | 0.657 | 0.736 | Summer peak, benefiting from July date |
| Almonds | 0.531 | 0.658 | Evergreen tree — needs spring leaf-out date |
| Walnuts | 0.506 | 0.667 | Deciduous tree — needs leaf-off/leaf-on contrast |
| Winter Wheat | 0.419 | 0.649 | Green-up Dec–Feb, senescence May — single July date misses crop entirely |
| Alfalfa | 0.374 | 0.595 | Multiple cuttings per year — high intra-class temporal variance |

Winter Wheat and Alfalfa show the largest single-date → multi-temporal gain, confirming that phenological diversity of dates is essential for spectrally similar crops.
